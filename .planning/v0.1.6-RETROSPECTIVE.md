## What changed mid-flight
- **FR1's discriminator was redesigned twice by research + advisor.** First-pass "a multi-line frontmatter list item ‚Üí block" would have wedged **every** legitimate blocker (a blocker is a 3-line `id`/`text`/`raisedAt` object). Corrected to a **field-specific, blacklist** check. And the "reuse `parseFrontmatter`" instinct was reversed to **raw-text** parsing ‚Äî `parseFrontmatter` throws on the exact malformed YAML the check targets, so a parse-first design would have failed *open* and missed its headline case.
- **FR2 stayed read-time ‚Äî but the reason changed.** DISCUSS assumed a PreToolUse hook *can't* warn non-blockingly; research proved it *can* (`systemMessage`). Read-time won anyway, on the stronger ground of zero cross-project blast radius.

## What assumptions broke
- "PreToolUse can't emit a non-blocking warning" ‚Äî false.
- "Introduce BUGS.md / build the hook" ‚Äî both already existed (extend, not create); footer-drift was already fixed in E10.S3 (a bookkeeping move, not a fifth fix). Scouting before framing caught all three.

## What surprised us
- The advisor caught a **ship-blocking FR1 bug hiding in the RESEARCH doc** (the blocker false-positive) before any code was written ‚Äî the single highest-leverage moment of the build.
- The drain's live effect was **43‚Üí37, not 43‚Üí36**: the 7th blockquote-stamped entry only *quotes* the stamp mid-body, and the `^`-anchored leading-line scan correctly left it live ‚Äî the exact over-match trap the anchor exists to prevent, confirmed on real data.
- REVIEW found FR1 was **inert on CRLF** files ‚Äî a real stranger-adoption gap (Windows `autocrlf`) that unit tests on LF fixtures never would have surfaced.

## What we'd do differently
- **Tracking a patch as `current_epic: v0.1.6` (a version string) rubs against Epic-shaped machinery** ‚Äî the SHIP FR1 milestone-derivation and `deriveRetroPath` both assume an `M{n}.E{n}` ID. It degraded gracefully (not-an-Epic-close ‚Üí skipped), but it's friction the committed **Epic-native flow** Epic is meant to remove.

## What to feed back into Signal
- The **field-specific + blacklist + "when-in-doubt-allow"** discriminator is a reusable pattern for any cross-project guard where a false positive wedges a stranger.
- **Research fans out to correct the spec, not just fill it** ‚Äî three of this build's best decisions were spec *corrections* surfaced by the parallel researchers + advisor, not confirmations.
- The version-as-`current_epic` friction is one more concrete vote for **Epic-native flow** (already the committed next Epic).

## Anti-rationalization moment
The PLAN FUTURE-IDEAS drain surfaced 43 candidates ‚Äî but the drain tool was the very thing FR3 was repairing, and 7 were false-positives it couldn't see. Rather than mass-disposition 43 legit items or churn on the false-positives, the drain was **deferred with a documented reason** (a convergent drain runs after FR3 ships), not silently skipped.
