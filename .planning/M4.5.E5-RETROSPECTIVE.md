## What changed mid-flight

The tester-brief's ask was narrowed from FR3's "run `/sig:init → /sig:ship` on real work" to the PLAN's scoped "~20-min `/sig:calibrate → /sig:discuss`, log the friction" — a deliberate refinement (a smaller first ask is likelier to actually get done), executed per the PLAN with the fuller flow offered as optional. Nothing else in scope moved; the 4-slice plan held end to end. The version number was correctly left unstamped (deferred to Brett at close, D-E5-4/5).

## What assumptions broke

The plan assumed the launch assets' dogfood numbers were accurate as cited. They weren't, fully: the README SKETCH-vs-FULL table claimed FULL = 14 `.planning/` artifacts, but the committed url-shortener example has 13 — and the launch post had inherited the wrong number. Caught in the S4.t9 sweep, reconciled in both places. The assumption that "cite the table" is safe is only as good as the table.

## What surprised us

How little of E5's value was outward prose and how much was internal release plumbing. Three of four slices (kit, CHANGELOG, sweep) produce nothing a stranger reads — they make a release *cuttable*. The Epic's real output is optionality: a release that can be cut on a whim or never, with the outward, irreversible actions cleanly fenced off into a handoff checklist.

## What we'd do differently

Run the cross-doc accuracy sweep against cited numbers *before* writing the prose that quotes them, not after. The 14→13 drift would have been caught at S1.t2 instead of surviving to S4.t9. A "verify the numbers you're about to quote" step belongs at the top of a docs slice, not the bottom.

## What to feed back into Signal

Two things. (1) A docs-Epic PLAN could carry a "claimed-facts ledger" — every number or claim a doc will assert, paired with its source-of-truth path, verified at slice start. (2) The version-decision rubric (this Epic's LAUNCH-KIT §1) is genuinely reusable but lives in a transient Epic artifact; a future Epic should promote it to a durable home (the deferred `docs/versioning.md`) so the CHANGELOG can link it permanently instead of referencing it in prose.

## Anti-rationalization moment

At the S2/S3 gate I surfaced a stale `.planning/` breadcrumb and a "drafts need a voice pass" note as bare observations and almost moved on — "flagging rather than churning." The user named it directly: Observations < Recommendations < Actions. Rather than rationalize the bare flags as good enough, the breadcrumb was fixed on the spot, a mechanical accuracy self-pass was run on all three drafts, and the working-norm memory was sharpened. The cheap, known defect got fixed instead of flagged — which is the whole point of the gate.
