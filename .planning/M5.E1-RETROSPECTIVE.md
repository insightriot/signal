## What changed mid-flight
- **S3 split into S3a/S3b** at EXECUTE, exactly as the VALIDATION watch-item predicted (the faithfulness gate was heavy enough to warrant separating the pure gate from the move orchestration).
- **The dogfood diverged from the plan's assumption.** RESEARCH assumed S5 would apply "S3's move-helper" to Signal's own body. It couldn't — `evictEpicNarrative` is Epic-*section*-scoped and Signal's body was one un-sectioned legacy blob (vector-2-existing). S5 relocated it with a one-off hand script instead; a general mechanism is now E3/FR6.

## What assumptions broke
- **"FR2a fixes the acute 455 KB case"** — wrong, and the research caught it: the acute case is vector-1 (frontmatter prose), which FR2a (a body mechanism) never touches. Routed to E3.
- **"The dogfood proves the whole Epic."** It proved FR3 on real data, but FR2b (`evictEpicNarrative`) was never invoked in S5 — the advisor caught this at the EXECUTE gate, and it reframed VERIFY's claims and REVIEW's focus.
- **"Tests passing means the flagship is sound."** The passing suite sailed past a coverage-gate-defeat bug (substring `includes`) and a silent data-loss bug (ledger key collision) — both found only by independent REVIEW.

## What surprised us
- **Clean `main` was red on entry** — the M4.5.E11 → v0.1.7 ship never bumped `plugin.json` (stuck at 0.1.6), so a v0.1.7 install self-reported 0.1.6. Cataloged as B7, fixed as standalone hygiene before S1.
- **A delegated executor stalled mid-stream (S4) after committing** — the work was durable and independently verified green; the failure was cosmetic (died during its final report).
- **The advisor's "name the blind spot" discipline paid off twice** — the faithfulness gate's paraphrase-distortion blind spot (documented, not papered over) and the FR2b-never-live-fired gap both became honest carry-forwards instead of silent over-claims.

## What we'd do differently
- **Live-fire the flagship in the dogfood.** S5 should have staged a synthetic closed-Epic body section so `evictEpicNarrative` fired at least once on non-fixture content. It didn't, so the flagship's first real firing is still ahead.
- **Reconcile MILESTONE-5.md's Epic numbering earlier.** The speculative `### M5.E1–E6` queue collided with the built M5.E1 the moment the override landed; catching it at DISCUSS rather than SHIP would have avoided a close-time reconciliation.
- **Bounded-match from the start.** The coverage gate's substring `includes` (I-1) was an avoidable class of bug — ID coverage should have been bounded on first write.

## What to feed back into Signal
- **The delegation-then-independently-verify pattern worked well** and is worth codifying: delegate mechanical slices to fresh executors, keep judgment-heavy slices (the gate, the dogfood) in main context, and never trust an executor's self-report — re-run the suite + review the diff.
- **REVIEW's independent-specialists pattern earns its cost** — it found a data-loss bug and a gate-defeat bug that VERIFY (which checks code, not adversarial inputs) could not.
- **"Name the blind spot" belongs in the retro template's spirit** — green tests ≠ proven; say what a green suite does and does not establish.

## Anti-rationalization moment
The temptation was to call the S5 dogfood "the doc-runtime dogfooded end-to-end" and move on. The advisor's EXECUTE-gate pass forced the honest reframe: **FR3 was dogfooded on real data; FR2b was proven on fixtures only, armed but never live-fired.** Every downstream artifact (VERIFICATION, REVIEW, this retro) now states that precisely rather than letting a green suite imply more than it earned.
